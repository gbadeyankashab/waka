Brussels Airline

The airline rules stipulates that there are two options:

  1. for passengers who already know when they want to fly

    2. Rebooking the trip to a later date.

    New start of travel: according to the original ticket validity or by 31 December 2020 at the latest (whichever is earlier)

  2. for passengers who do not yet know the new date of travel and/or destination

    3. Rebooking/reissue of the existing ticket must be completed on/before 31 August 2020 for the new travel dates and the new routing.

    New start of travel: according to the original ticket validity or by 31 December 2020 at the latest (whichever is earlier)

Please see below the details of policy:
  • Eligible for passengers with a booked flight until 31 December 2020, and
  • With an SN (082) ticket issued on/before 31 March 2020, and
  • With a booked Brussels Airlines flight number operated by Brussels Airlines or any other code-share partner worldwide.
  • Passenger may rebook once free of charge and receive a credit or reduction of 50 Euro.
The following rules apply for both options:
  • The rebooking/reissue transaction must be completed on/before 31 August 2020
  • The rebooking fee does not apply
  • Origin and destination of the journey can be changed
  • New commencement of travel: according to the original ticket validity or by 31 December 2020 at the latest (whichever is earlier)
  • Fare difference has to be charged, but If the total price (fare + taxes/fees/surcharges) of the new ticket is higher than the total price of the existing ticket, the additional payment is reduced by EUR 50
  • If the total price of the new ticket is lower than the total price of the existing ticket, the customer will receive a residual value EMD of up to EUR 50, which can be refunded.
  • Change of the passenger ("Name Change") is not permitted
  • The conditions of the new fare apply
  • These rebookings/reissues are also permitted for PNRs/tickets that have already been rebooked/reissued (earlier reissues based on a goodwill rule or due to cancelled flights)
  • The goodwill policy also applies to passengers who have been a no-show on their originally booked flight.
  • EMDs that were associated to the original ticket remain valid.

FAQs

  1. What are EMD Vouchers:

    2. EMD vouchers are Electronic Miscellaneous Document issued via the airline with the same value of an original ticket paid to the airline which can be used by same passengers to purchase another ticket with same airline for a future date.

    The vouchers are usually valued for one year or based on the initial validity of your original ticket and can be used to buy a new ticket on any future travel date.

  2. What are Open Tickets:

    3. Most airlines have allowed passengers to leave tickets open allowing the customer to make a change in the future. Not all airlines allow one free change on your flight ticket, which gives customers time to decide after the pandemic to utilize the flight ticket.

  3. Status on Refunds:

    4. A recent open letter by International Air Transport Association (IATA) on refunds by the airline states that; “Our industry is experiencing a critical liquidity crisis. Most airlines are spending more cash in reimbursing their passengers than they receive in new booking revenues. We recently estimated the industry’s liability in this area at $35 billion. In this context, airlines’ most urgent need is to keep their remaining liquidity to pay salaries and face their fixed 2 costs. It is practically impossible for industry players to find sufficient financial means to keep the air travel value chain operating in the short time that airlines have before facing bankruptcy.”

    We have seen delays in refunds process from the airline which we can no longer determine when the funds will be paid to the company to reimburse our customers, hence we have been advised to offer customers EMD vouchers or leave the tickets open for a future travel
